§ Mancala


How to play

The goal of mancala is to move stones from your side of the board into your goal. The bottom player moves stones from the bottom six holes to the right goal, and the top player moves stones from the top six holes to the left goal. The player with the most stones in their goal at the end of the game wins.

To move, choose one of the six holes on your side of the board. The stones in that hole are then picked up, and dropped off one at a time in the next holes (moving clockwise). The game ends when one of the players no longer has any stones on their side of the board.

Special cases:

• If on your move the last stone dropped is in your goal, then you can take another turn.

• If the last stone dropped is in an empty hole on your side, and there are stones in your opponents hole opposite you, then the stones are "captured". Your last stone and your opponent's captured stones are all placed in your goal.

• When the game ends, one player will likely still have stones on their side of the board. These remaining stones count towards their score.

Of course, the easiest way to understand these rules is to try playing the game yourself! Go ahead and try it out at /play.


The game state

Like all games at AI Tourney, the game state is stored in JavaScript Object Notation (JSON). This is what the mancala JSON looks like:

{
 "id": (number), // The gameID
 "game": "mancala",
 "players": [
  {
   "username": (string)
   "pos": (0 or 1) // The player's position (bottom==0, top==1)
  },
  { ... } // Same object for player two
 ],
 "board": [
   4, 4, 4, 4, 4, 4, 0, // [0-6] are bottom 6 holes and goal
   4, 4, 4, 4, 4, 4, 0, // [7-11] are top 6 holes and goal
 ],
 "currentPlayer": (0 or 1), // Whether it is player0's or player1's turn
 "gameOver": (0 or 1), // Whether the game is over (1) or not (0)
 "winner": (-1 or 0 or 1), // The winner player (-1 for no winner yet)
 "error": (string), // If any error occured it should show up here
}

Your move

Making your move is fairly simple in mancala. Just print the index number of the hole of the stones that you want to move. For the bottom player, that would be indices 0 - 5. For the top player, that would be indices 7 - 12.
